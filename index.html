FAQ

common questions

What is GSV? +

GSV is a personal AI computer. Instead of running one AI agent on one machine, it connects all your devices (laptop, home server, phone) into a single computer with an AI in the kernel. You talk to it from wherever you already are — whether that's Telegram, Discord, or a terminal — and it acts across every machine at once. The brain lives on the edge, so it keeps working even when your devices are asleep.

What can I do with it? +

Hand it the admin you don't want to do, and let it act across all your machines at once. A few of the things people reach for first:

  • Delegate the tedious stuff. Email triage, scheduling, travel admin, the busywork of being alive. It keeps working while your laptop is closed, because the brain runs on the edge.
  • Reach any device from anywhere. Read a log on your home server from your phone, then push from your laptop, in the same conversation. They all act as one computer.
  • Talk to it where you already are. Telegram, Discord, a terminal — whichever you live in.
  • Write and share your own programs. GSV is programmable, with its own apps that anyone can write, run, and share between instances.

It's a computer, not a chatbot, so the real answer is 'whatever you can build on it'.

What does it actually cost? +

Two costs, kept separate so you can do the math:

  • Infra: GSV runs in your own Cloudflare account and needs the Workers Paid plan, which starts at ~$5/mo.
  • Models: you bring your own model provider and pay them directly for what you use.

There's no GSV subscription on top. You pay Cloudflare and your model provider, nobody in between.

Do I need a Cloudflare account? +

Yes. GSV runs on Cloudflare's edge, in your account. That's how the brain stays always-on and how your keys and data stay yours. You'll need the Workers Paid plan (~$5/mo). If you don't have an account yet, setup is part of the Get Started guide.

Is it really open source? +

Yes, MIT licensed, all the code is public: github.com/deathbyknowledge/gsv. Read every line yourself.

One honest distinction: open source is true today. Full self-hosting off Cloudflare is on the roadmap, not shipped. Today GSV runs in your own Cloudflare account, with your keys and your data, rather than fully on your own metal. The code is all there; running it off Cloudflare just isn't supported yet.

How is this different from running an agent on a VPS? +

An agent on a VPS is one brain in one place. It lives on the single host you picked and manage. GSV is a computer that spans every device you own at once. It can read a log on your home server and push from your laptop in the same breath, even when those devices are off, because the brain runs on the edge rather than on any one box. Different shape, not just a different agent.

Is my data private? +

Your keys and your data live in your own Cloudflare account and are never routed through us. Devices connect outbound through a gateway with tokens: no open ports, no VPN, no exposed box. We're not 'infrastructure you own' in the bare-metal sense (Cloudflare is in the path), but nothing passes through GSV the company.

Is it ready? What's the catch? +

It's beta, and we build in the open. The distributed, always-on core works, and that's the part we're launching. The rough edges right now are onboarding polish and the off-Cloudflare self-hosting story (roadmap, not shipped). We'd rather tell you that up front than have you find it. Getting in now means getting in at the ground floor.
